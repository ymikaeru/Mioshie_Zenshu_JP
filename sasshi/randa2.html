　　　Truth, Virtue, Beauty

　　The Earthly Paradise, our ideal, is a world where truth, virtue and beauty shall reign supreme.
　　Let us explain the meaning of the above more fully.
　　First, on truth. This means, of course, realization of the truth, and truth is things as they really are without even the slightest deviation. However, in civilization to date, things which are not the truth have been taken for the truth, that is, so many pseudo-truths have passed current with the world as the truths. The reason why people have not noticed their error is that the level of science in general has been too low.
　　The situation of the modern world in which people only toil and struggle to earn their livelihood with no definite hope, testifies to the above fact. They proudly allude to the advanced civilized world in spite of the fact that they live under the menace of disease, hard living conditions and war.
　　From an impartial viewpoint, the modern phase of life in which people are at variance with each other, quarrel with each other, devote themselves to strife, thus struggling in the vortex of uneasiness and irritation, is just brutal. This has resulted from the civilization in which pseudo truths dominate. It is very pitiful that even intelligentsia can not recognize their errors, believe the modern world as "civilized" and sing in praise of it.
　　In regards disease, the world is filled with the sick, because medical science is deviated front the truth. Why does there exist innumerable sorts of disease, e.g., tuberculosis, dysentery, Japanese encephalitis B, apoplectic stroke, polio, and so on? One excuse for this is as follows: there existed many types of diseases in the olden times, but they were not classified because of lack of advancement of medical science, but now, advanced science has made it possible to discern each kind of disease.
　　Be that as it may, the only hope is to decrease the sick and increase the healthy in number.
　　Modern people fear disease to such an extent that medical specialists as well as the government authorities feel very nervous about hygiene, and are making strenuous effort in the prevention of diseases. One method is by injection of drugs, which, I think, is rather pathetic. Injection of drugs in general will not cure disease, but only relieve patients temporarily. Thus medical science, can not even tell the difference between temporary relief and fundamental cure. If they did know it, they would not be able to know how to cure. Medical specialists only seek to halt the outward symptoms of disease and believe that it testifies to the progress of medical science.
　　It may be beyond their imagination that the very method used in halting the symptoms of disease is nothing but the cause of successive diseases. Thus, it is quite natural that the more medical science advances, the more the number of the sick increase. Take the present situation of the world in which the sick keep increasing and the general tendency of health conditions is on the down-slope. Hence, people are afraid of fatigue and insufficient sleep, loss of stamina, they can not overtax themselves and are exhausted quickly by excessive physical exercise.
　　Physical exercise or sports which are recently being encouraged will also be in vain as a method to promote health. Not a few athletes are short lived.
　　Those who adhere to the principle of the pathology which the Church of World Messianity advocates, and receive "Johrei" or Soul Purification Treatment, will be released from the curse of disease and enjoy health in the true sense of the word. Innumerable instances testify to the genuineness of this.
　　As for virtue, it is contrary to vice. Vice originated from atheism which is a product of materialism, while virtue originated from theism. This is the truth. Increase of vice accompanies the progress of science, and so to speak, progress of civilization is just superficial as the general tendency of science is inclined to deny theism. Vice which accompanies the progress of science should not be thought little of although the merit of science can not be denied.
　　Scientists, being ignorant of this, only praise the meritorious side of science, and as to the harmful side of it, they have their own excuses, thus captivating the mind of intelligentsia and causing them to believe that science alone is capable of solving every problem.
　　This has led people away from attaining spiritual happiness.
　　Next comes beauty. It is a very good tendency indeed that the element of beauty has come to occupy greater part in our civilization in accord with the progress of culture, but the mass of the people are not able to participate in the enjoyment of beauty. The multitude are fully occupied in struggling to live whereas only a privileged class of people are enjoying the pleasures that beauty can bring.
　　Far from enjoying beauty, the masses can only obtain the necessities of life, especially so in Japan. Thus it is almost impossible for them to enjoy the beauty of nature which is the great bounty of God, and art which is man-made beauty.
　　It is not the whole people of the world who can share in the benefit of the advanced civilization, but only a part of them. So this world is heavenly only for the rich while it is miserable for the poor. It follows therefore that there must be something wrong with the modern culture. The world will become a true civilized world when its fault has been rectified and all the people impartially are able to enjoy happiness.
　　The mission of the Church of World Messianity is to realize such a civilized world in its truest sense. Now, I think, the meaning of "truth, virtue and beauty" has been clearly defined. The question is the power to realize fulfilment. An ideal is futile if it is not realized.
　　However, the time has come when such an ideal is to be realized on earth.



　　　Mission of Art

　　Everything in this world has its own mission which renders its service to human society. This is what is called the "Mission of Heaven."
　　Of course, art is also placed under this category. Those engaged in art, therefore, must be conscious of their mission of heavenly gift and fulfil it completely. Therein lies "art" in its true sense, and at the same time it is what an artist really is.
　　However, regrettable is the fact that artists in general of the present day are deviating too far from the right path though some of them, are undoubtedly artists in the true sense.
　　For the most part, artists have forgotten their own mission －or it may be rather proper to say that they have never borne it in mind. Despite this, they consider themselves to be a special sort of people in society, and behave as they please in the belief that it is a display of individuality and also a manifestation of genius to act so. This is, indeed, a matter for regret.
　　Be that as it may, the responsibility is partly found on the part of society. It must be remembered that such degradation of artists is caused by the fact that society has treated them as people of a special class, and overlook their faults.
　　To speak orthodoxically, those who are called artists must originally have a nobler character than ordinary people. This will be understandably accounted for from the religious point of view.
　　Historical facts reveal that the primitive age was characterized by brutality, which constituted most of the element of mankind. We wonder if there is any one who doubts that "ideal culture" has been in the process of construction through all stages from these barbaric times. In view of this, development or culture is nothing but the removing of brutality from human beings, which will pave the way for the civilized world in the true sense of the words.
　　It is deplorable, however, that most of human beings are still exposed to the menace of war. This is because brutality still remains as an element within them. To assume one of the important duties to remove this brutality is the very task imposed upon artists.
　　Accordingly, the primary mission of an artist is to raise the spiritual character of all human beings. Its object will be attained through the means of literature, paintings, music, plays, etc. It is that the spirit of an artist appeals to the public through the offices of such mediums. To speak plainly, the inspiration from the spirit of an artist awakes the responsive cord in the heart of the masses through literature, paintings, music and the art of the dance etc. Eventually it will mean the linking of the spirit of an artist and that of the masses.
　　Therefore, it the character of an artist is low, the effect upon the people is also relative. It is also quite natural that if the character of an artist is high, the sentiment of the public should be uplifted also. That is why art is valuable. In short, an artist is a leader of the masses who should be guided to a better and finer realization of life through art.



　　　A Remark on Art

　　The tendency of art in general, especially paintings of the modern school, is drifting away from the true mission wherein art should lie. Painters are neglecting for the most part this all important mission.
　　Artists should be aware of the significance of their existence. Their duty is to elevate people to a higher spiritual level. To elevate the human mind to a more virtuous, beautiful condition. This is the mission of painting in its truest sense. Free exertion of a personality, or volition for purposes of creation, or free choice of subjects are quite all right, but beyond a certain limitation, they make no sense.
　　In modern paintings, there are many pictures which are so grotesque that we can not find even a trace of beauty in them, and so deformed that they are even offending.
　　Such paintings are not an expression of personality, they are rather a means of forcing sales. The effect of such pictures is far from elevating spiritually. It is interesting to note that painters of such pictures are usually in needy circumstances, despite their efforts, and suffering from dire poverty, and without contributing to the world.
　　Above is my opinion on modern painting, and there are not a few people who agree with this opinion and criticize similarly. The true life or painting is elegance and tone. Such elements are scarcely contained in modern painting.
　　Whenever I see masterpieces of Chinese or Japanese paintings, I am struck with their high tone. Modern Japanese painters are almost indifferent to this quintessence of Eastern painting. We can find this quintessence in the paintings by old masters, but young painters seem to follow in the wake of the modern school.
　　The true significance of art in general, including Japanese painting or Western painting, is this; to deepen man's intelligence and lead his soul to a higher level by conveying the author's spirit through the senses. If its purpose is only to amuse, then it can not be said to be art. The same thing can be applicable to fine art, literature, music, drama, the dance etc. To appeal to the popular mind through art, eliminate brutality from man's character, and to make man more civilized in the true sense, these are raison d'etre of art. The more objectivity an art object has, therefore, the more valuable it is as such. However high value an author attaches to his own work, it is valueless if it does not pass current with the world.
　　Let us look at the great works by the great masters since olden times. Their divine skill which is so comprehensive that it amuses and charms the intelligentsia as well as the multitude is vividly alive still today.
　　Most modern art is a food which is tasty and without nutrition. A show which only satisfies one's passing interest. Such art only serves to deteriorate people's character.



　　　Religion and Art

　　The common idea that religion and art are almost alien to each other is erroneous. The mission of art is to created nobler sentiment in people, make their life more significant and have them enjoy their life. It gives us inexpressible pleasure to see flowers in spring, crimson foliage in autumn, beautiful scenery of mountains and sea, and enjoy literary works or art objects.
　　The Earthly Paradise, the realization of which we aim at, is a world of art or a world where truth, virtue and beauty dominate. Thus art is a realized form of beauty.
　　Why is it that art has been neglected by religionists to date? The genius of some high priests in the olden times towards beauty had so full swing that they painted, engraved, and planned the architecture of temples.
　　One of the most excellent religious artists was Prince Shotoku. All may agree with my opinion that the architecture of the Horyuji Temple at Nara, planned by Prince Shotoku, or paintings and sculptures contained in it are so splendid, that the fact that it was built about one thousand two hundred years ago is almost unimaginable.
　　On the other hand, however, there were many saints and great priests who have propagated teachings, being content with coarse clothing and poor food, thus leading an ascetic life. This fact has given birth to the common idea that art and religion are merely distantly related. Their lives may have been dominated by truth and virtue, but were lacking in beauty.
　　In such a sense, I shall advocate the necessity of art.



　　　Artistic Religion

　　Since olden times, art has been deemed as something apart from religion. However, the origin of art in Japan was Buddhistic art. The keynote of Buddhistic art is simple in its character. For example, painting, sculpture and textile fabric are most simple, and as for music, musical instruments are simple and the melodies played by them are also very simple. Then stimulated by Chinese and Korean art which were later imported, unique Japanese art was created after the period of imitative art. With the importation of Western culture, Western art also came in. Especially, after the Meiji Era, Japan became flooded with the art of Europe and America. So in the modern artistic world of Japan, was created a synthetic art by digesting masterpieces of Western Countries. In such sense, Japan may be called a department store of world culture.
　　There will be and has been no religion in the world which estimates art in general so highly as the Church of World Messianity. The Earthly Paradise which World Messianity is aiming at is a world of art. As the Earthly Paradise is a world free from disease, poverty and strife and where truth, virtue and beauty dominate, in such a world, men follow the truth, detest vice and thus, everything in that world is beautified. As a result, people become fond of art and their life is filled with art. Art prospers and makes all the world artistic, or the world of art.
　　Therefore, I am most interested in art, and shall patronize art. As its first step, I have built the miniature Earthly Paradise at Gohra, Hakone.

　　　The Shinsenkyo Garden

　　Although there are many celebrated gardens in Japan which have their individual characteristics, they are not so remarkable for their uniqueness. However, I believe the Shinsenkyo is an exception.
　　An astonishing amount of rocks and stone of strange shapes were dug from around the vicinity, and I arranged these without following the traditional make-up of the typical Japanese garden in order to create an utterly new form following divine guidance. I have also collected various kinds of trees to create a harmonious effect. As for the streams and falls, I have tried to produce the merit of natural art, intensifying their natural flavour and tying the beauty of nature with that of the garden. Thus, I have tried to awaken the idea of beauty in man's mind through the joy which a beautiful garden brings.
　　Whether the garden is looked at close up, from a distance, partly or in whole, each case presents a different aspect. In the course of time, the various kinds of moss, small grasses, flowers or small trees which are peculiar to Hakone, have grown in the hollows of the rocks and recently the garden has acquired a look of age which has added to its beauty.
　　The charm of the garden gives one a feeling of attachment to the locality. Following the rain one is fascinated by the noise of the meandering mountain streams.
　　The two falls of the stream represent well the harmonious beauty of nature and art and has been a source of satisfaction to me. One may be reminded in gazing at them of mountain recesses or dark valleys. The crimson foliage around is in good contrast with the falls, and there is depth in general tone of the setup.
　　It may sound not a little conceited to praise that which I have constructed myself, but actually this is not so. Although l made the garden. God made it beautiful, and I am singing in the praise or God.
　　The museum which has recently been completed adds to the beauty of the garden. Part of the art objects exhibited in it are my own collection, but I have checked up on the national treasures owned by other museums, personal collections and art objects in temples in Japan and intend to exhibit them at some time in our museum. My plan is to exhibit the new as well as the masterpieces of Eastern art by the masters of each period, chiefly from the aesthetic point of view. Only a small part will be chosen from the historical and archaeological point of view.



　　　Zuiunkyo As a World-widely Noted Place

　　The model Earthly Paradise at Momoyama, Atami which is under construction, was at first known as the finest in the Kanto District, then grandest in Japan with the progress of the construction work, and recently it is called one of the most unique places in the world. There are many noted religious architectures in the world, like, the Parthenon in Greece, Vatican in Rome, Westminster Abbey in England, etc.
　　As non-religious architecture, Peking Castle is noted for its vast scale and the magnificence of its architectural beauty. In Japan, Toshogu at Nikko will be the only place which we can boast of as such. Compared with the places mentioned above, the amount of money which was used in building Zuiunkyo or Atami Earthly Paradise will be relatively small.
　　However, in regards to the location on which it is built, its completeness, the beautiful scenery around it or commanded from it, it will have no equal in the world. Many architectural specialists have praised it unanimously from this viewpoint.
　　First its natural environment. The hill on which it stands is only about one hundred meters above sea level. And it is easily accessible.
　　The Messianic Hall which is situated on the site which is higher, is 108 feet wide and 120 feet deep and its floor space is about 35,604 square feet. I have planned it on a newer architectural style based on that of Le Corbusier, famous French architect. The design is very simple.
　　Interesting enough, this Le Corbusier style was born, I hear, from a hint taken from the architectural style of a Japanese tea house. At one side of this building there is a stone wall of about 24 feet high and about 300 feet long. This stone wall alone is astonishing in its magnitude.
　　Only God could prepare such line of beauty of the undulating hills which surround the hall. Looked as a whole from the foot of the hill, one will be so fascinated by the beauty that one may forget that one is in this world.
　　Besides, one hundred old plum trees, one hundred Cherry trees, which originated in Yoshino, Kyoto, fifty cherry trees bearing double blossoms, less than one hundred large azaleas, and chinchoges or daphne odoras, lilacs, wistarias, yellow roses, camellias, tulips, hyacinth, daffodils, chrysanthemum coronariums, anemones, pansies, carnations, and cyclamens, etc., all spring flowering plants will be planted. So when it is completed, its beauty will be beyond imagination and incomparable. Above is the first part of the construction work. When the second and third parts of the construction work have been completed, I believe that this Model Earthly Paradise will become an internationally noted place. Then for all the visitors from abroad, visiting this place will be a must, with that of the Toshogu in Nikko.
　　Finally, the purpose of constructing such building is to create a work of art which is a combination of art and the beauty of nature of Japan. For that purpose, choice of position is most important. Therefore, I traveled all over Japan before I made my decision. As a result, I found that Atami is an ideal place, where the climate is very mild, and which is full of variety with hotsprings, mountains, sea, islands (Hatsu-shima Island and Ohshima Island) and a winding coast-line.
　　Besides it is situated between the Kansai District and Kanto District. This makes the place easy of access. It also adjoins Hakone and Izu Penninsula, two national parks. Thus it may be said to have been chosen by divine guidance. I was able to purchase the most beautiful place in Atami which amounts to 30,000 tsubo. Thus every condition to build the model of the Earthly Paradise in Atami may be said to have been prepared earlier－by God.



　　　Divine Help Received

　　I am firmly convinced that the building of the Hakone Art Museum, was achieved by something more than merely human power. The speed with which it was built and the quickness with which art objects owned by it have been gathered have no precedent. Such speed is so remarkable that it could be called a divine skill. Private museums in this country have been completed by millionaire virtuosos after efforts of a whole lifetime, although I do not know if it is the same in foreign countries.
　　The Hakone Art Museum has been completed, however, only in several years since it was first planned. The money necessary came as donations from our followers, and the art objects owned by the museum have been collected after World War II or borrowed, not particularly being sought for by us for the definite purpose of establishing the Hakone Art Museum. The only way of construction has been to trust in God's skill. The same thing can be said of the variety of the kinds of the art objects.
　　I was able to purchase art objects which I was anxious to get for considerably cheap prices and also some of them were donated by followers. Thus art objects enough to be exhibited in the museum have been collected quite smoothly and naturally. Everything went so well that it was as if God had a hand in the work.
　　I have also collected ancient art objects of Egypt, Greece, Persia, India and old China etc. so abundantly that they can hardly all be exhibited at one time. I have never dreamed of collecting these. I have not even known the ancient art of the West. However, those subjects began to come in one after another this year. I was very happy that they have been collected. By and by I have perceived their charm and so I was struck by the divine intention working in the collection of those ancient art objects of Western countries. The Art Museum took the initiative in collecting them because they have never been collected by other museums in Japan including the National Museum.
　　When I visted the Ukiyo-e Exhibition held by the Kyoto Museum of Art last year, I perceived that Ukiyo-e, in which I had not been previously much interested, was wonderful and then I was introduced a Dr. Ichitaro Kondo, famous scholar of Ukiyo-e who was kind enough to explain about Ukiyo-e to me. This contributed to my getting a general idea of Ukiyo-e. Later my knowledge on Ukiyo-e was increased. Then, excellent masterpieces of Ukiyo-e which were hitherto hard to obtain, began to pour in without asking. This assured me of the Divine Will. The main building of the museum had not enough space for another exhibition, so I planned a wing and held an Ukiyo-e exhibition. The wing after its completion enhanced the beauty of the museum.

　　　Socialization of Art

　　Basic account will be given here of why I have established an art museum on the hill of Hakone in Shizuoka Prefecture, a well-known tourist point in Japan.
　　As is usually stated, the primary object of World Messianity is to create a world in which truth, virtue and beauty are united in perfect harmony. The museum symbolizes beauty of the three. It is, so to speak, an unprecedented work of art in which natural and man made beauty are united to the utmost.
　　Japan possesses many fine works of art as do other countries in the world. However, such works of art have so far been in the hands of the privileged classes and not displayed to the general public. They have been shown only to a limited few. In other words, it is the monopolization of art, representing a feudalistic trend of thinking to which Japanese have ever adhered.
　　Regretting this matter, I planned to break down this evil and socialize art. In short, I desired to emancipate art and give it life.
　　Fortunately, my long-cherished desire has materialized earlier than expected as a result of earnest cooperation of my religious followers.
　　There are many private art museums throughout the country, but their original intention far differed from mine. These private museums have been brought into incorporated bodies by millionaires or financial cliques who wish to maintain and safeguard a number of works of art which they have collected, regardless of expenses, merely to satisfy their taste, protect their property and attain their aspiration after fame. The art galleries are perfunctorily opened for a short period twice in spring and autumn in accordance with the regulations providing that they must be exhibited to the public for a fixed number of days a year. Accordingly, it must be said that their social significance is very remote.
　　Contrarily, our museum at Hakone is always opened to the public except for the three months of December, January and February, in which it is closed because of bad weather.
　　Even from this viewpoint, the Hakone museum is idealistic. Furthermore, there are many rare and well-known works of art being displayed in it, which it is sure to give great satisfaction to those who are interested in art. I have purposely kept the admission as low as possible. The museum will render great service even in social welfare.
　　National museums are not so useful to those studying contemporary art. since they are, for the most part, occupied by historical and archaeological works of art. Private museums in this country mostly constitute works of Chinese and Western art. In this sense, there have been no museums characteristic of Japanese art in this country before the completion of our gallery at Hakone. The Hakone Museum, therefore, will contribute greatly even from the viewpoint of preserving valuable cultural assets.
　　It has been remarked by President Asano of the National Museum at Ueno, and Chief Fujikawa of the General Affairs Bureau of the Cultural Properties Protection Commission that such a museum as ours is meeting the demands of the State perfectly. They also stated that they wished me to give my full efforts to the work, as they greatly favoured it.
　　The prospect that foreign tourists visiting Japan will drop in at the Hakone Museum, is very gratifying. Even from this viewpoint, the museum will not a little contribute toward enhancing the status of Japanese culture in the world. Beginning with Dr. Warner, many noted foreigners have already visited the Hakone museum.
　　I am confident that the day will not be distant when the museum will become internationally known and counted as one of the features of Japan.



　　　Paradise Is a World of Beauty

　　Followers of Wold Messianity well know that the purpose of God is to create an ideal world where truth, virtue and beauty dominate. And, the purpose of Satan is, needless to say, to foster falsehood, vice and ugliness. "Falsehood" and "vice" may be understood, but "ugliness" may need explaining.
　　One mistake has been passing current with the world, that is, people have been apt to accept ugliness when it goes hand in hand with truth and virtue. Indeed, there have been noble persons who have practised virtue for people and society, being content with coarse clothing, simple foods, humble dwellings, thus leading a life of utter simplicity. Should they be forced by circumstances to live thus, it can not be helped, but if not, and they still like to lead such a plain life it is not good.
　　Many religionists have led ascetic lives as a form of self-discipline. Such way of life has been deemed respectable by themselves and also by people in general. This way of thinking is however, erroneous. An important element of beauty is neglected and so, such a life is dominated by truth, virtue and ugliness. In such a sense, man's food, clothing and habitation should be as attractive and comfortable as possible so far as within his means. God approves such way of living. Beauty which not only satisfies oneself but gives pleasure to others may be looked upon as a kind of virtue.
　　The higher the civilization of a society the more it is conscious of the value of beauty. For example, there is scarcely a trace of beauty in the life of barbarians. So that we can say that progress of culture means at the same time progress of appreciation of beauty. As to individuals, even men should try to attain beauty enough to give a pleasure to others. It goes without saying that woman should try to become as beautiful as possible.
　　As for the home, beauty and orderliness should herein be stressed together with cleanliness. Not only the families living in such homes, but also guests will reel comfortable. Especially the latter will involuntarily feel respect towards the master of the home.
　　The exterior of a house, if it is kept clean and in good repair, will give a sense of pleasure to passers-by. I believe that Switzerland gives great pleasure to foreigners as her cities and parks are so lovely and clean. The very reason that visitors to that country are so impressed, should be an object lesson in itself.
　　We Japanese should cultivate more aesthetic sense of values. As a result, not only individuals, but also this country as a whole will unexpectedly benefit by it. Besides, beautiful environment makes for better social morals, and places man's mind on a higher plane, and this in turn helps to reduce crime and evil in society. Such phenomena will much contribute to realize the Earthly Paradise.
　　As for myself, I have liked anything concerned with beauty, and even whilst suffering from poverty, I used to make a small flower bed in my garden, paint, try to visit museums and exhibitions as often as possible, enjoy flowers in spring and the crimson foliage in autumn. Now I have become able enough through God's favour, to enjoy beauty at will, and this in turn contributes to the Divine Program. Those who are ignorant of this may criticize my life as being too extravagant, but this can not be helped. Compared with the founders of religions since olden days who spread their teachings while leading humble and penitent lives my mode of living may seem to be peculiar. Religionists used to spread their teachings while they themselves lived in hellish conditions, because those were the days which were the Nocturnal Periods in human history.
　　However, now is the transitional point when the Daylight Period is dawning upon the world, in which salvation of mankind will be performed. It should be realized that any idea which aims at an ideal and neglects beauty is false.

　　　Story of My Art Study

　　As I have previously stated, I have been interested in art since my younger days.
　　During the pre-war days, I was merely a student spectator and enjoyed viewing various works of art. To speak more frankly, there was no choice for me but to be satisfied only in visiting museums, art exhibitions or art shows in department stores, because of lack of funds.
　　After World War II, however, I managed to obtain possession of a number of works of art, at relatively low cost. But, what I purchased was limited to a specialized sort of works, for at that time I did not have sufficient knowledge in other fields of art. My pieces included drawings by Korin and Sotatsu, one-time famous artists in Japan, earthenware by also one-time well-known potters in Japan including Ninsei, Kanzan and Nabeshima, and picture scrolls. As to picture scrolls, I had once studied this work myself when I was young.
　　Thus, with my collection increasing, an idea came across my mind that if a world of truth, virtue and beauty, that is, the earthly paradise, which is advocated by World Messianity is to be created, beauty would be essential. Though truth and virtue are each of spiritual elements, beauty is material. If the beauty is to be displayed concretely, not only natural beauty but also man-made beauty will have to go with it. The best way to attain this is to set up an art museum.
　　Fortunately, as soon as I moved to Hakone in the spring of 1944, I found a suitable place nearby and bought it. Shortly after, I chanced to find a very nice place in Atami and secured it, also. Thus, with the securing of space, has developed the conception as is seen at the present. This evidences the profound plan of God which is shaping step by step. The art museum, whose completion is a God-given task has received its finishing touch.
　　In this connection, I will disclose a very interesting story. As stated earlier, I had no knowledge of art except for a specialized kind of works. However, God opened my eyes and increased my thirst for further knowledge. In the first one or two years, I got various pictorial records concerning drawings by the Korin school and works by potters including Ninsei, Kanzan and Nabeshima, at the same time, I had opportunities to hear experts explain them. When I managed to understand the substance of the works, I went into the study of modern pictures, the Yamato-e painting, a picture representing things Japanese, or the Ukiyo-e, a genre picture in the following year, then into the Higashiyama brush painting in ink (sumi), old brush writing or the pictures painted in Sung and Yuan Dynasties in China the next year, followed by potteries of China and Korea and Buddhist painting during the last year. With the coming of the new year, I chanced to obtain various bibliographies and pictorial records relating to Buddhist images. Meanwhile, I have had chances to get even Buddhist statues shown.
　　According to my experiences up to the present, it has taken me about one year to finish studying each field of art.
　　Such being the case, the works of art to be displayed at the Hakone Museum will run over various fields of art. I believe such an exhibition will be the first of its kind in Japan.
　　It comes as a surprise that there are no other art museums peculiar to Japan in this country. This is evident upon visiting other museums now existing in Japan. In the national museums, for instance, Buddhist art is surely worth seeing, but other fields of art to speak without reserve, are very poor. Even the Bridgestone Museum recently established in Japan is nothing but an art hall to represent Western paintings. The Okura Shuko Hall exists only for Chinese art, the Nezu Museum for pottery and Chinese copperware, the Kyoto Museum for temple art, the Sumitomo Art Gallery for Chinese copperware, the Hakutsuru Museum in Osaka for Chinese earthenware and copperware, and the Ohara Art Hall in Okayama for Western art.
　　It is indeed a matter of regret that we who are Japanese can not enjoy any works of art peculiar to Japan in our own country. From this viewpoint, I intend through the Hakone Museum to concentrate my energies upon Japanese art and give satisfaction to all. Though the museum is not large in scale yet, it will be sure to make those who visit it experience every satisfaction with art of Japan and, at the same time, be a source of interest to foreign visitors.



　　　Singularity of Japanese Culture

　　I should like all Japanese to realize the trait of their own national character, which had they previously known, their country would not have been plunged into war, nor would they have suffered such a miserable defeat. Just as self recognition is important to every individual, Japanese should know what Japan is. It is very necessary to know one's own country, as now is not the time of national isolation, but the time in which everything which occurs is international and world-wide in character and mutual relation. It is necessary to know what role this country should play.
　　The definite line on which this country should proceed can not be established unless the Japanese realize the raison d'etre of their own country. Until the end of World War II, a privileged military class formed a dictatorship, and an arbitrary regime was formed under the minority. The Japanese people had no voice against the men of power, and were enslaved. This is still fresh in our memory.
　　Since the Meiji Era, National Constitution and a representative system had been established and the will of the people was apparently respected, but in reality, the reins of power were held by a minority who led the people to war. This was like the case of crying wine and selling vinegar.
　　To review Japanese history. There have been constant internal disturbances in this country since the regime of Emperor Jimmu, the first emperor of Japan, and the government had been utterly controlled by the sword. An individual who showed skill in fighting was empowered in the name of chivalry, and the conqueror in war became supreme ruler of the time. Japan until the end of the war had been led by such historical current in which violence ruled. The great shock of defeat in World War II stopped the current.
　　The true line which Japan should take as a country of peace will not be established until all Japanese perceive deeply the significance of such Japanese history.
　　An important thing to stress again, is the true character of Japan. The character of Japan should be a country of peace in which art flourishes, altogether contrary to the role she had taken as a country of feudalism and militarism. Herein lies the mission which has been endowed Japan by God. Therefore, the reconstruction of this country which is so frequently talked about is nothing, if it does not allude to an entirely new Japan in character. Reconstructed Japan as popularly spoken about, will merely be a democratic shell without preparedness. To an extent this may be all right, but the Japanese people should perceive more clearly the special role of their country in the world, and by doing so, try to contribute to the welfare of all the people of the world. This is the true role which new Japan should play. Let us go deeper into this.
　　Most foreigners who visit this country praise her scenery which is of uncomparable excellence. The fact that Japan's climate is very distinctly divided into four seasons has a deep meaning. This causes distinct relative changes in her scenery. Mr. Kyoshi Takahama, a famous poet composer of seventeen syllable verse (Haiku), following his world tour, stated that no other country he visited had so clearly defined four seasonal changes, and as a true seventeen syllable poem applies to the seasons, it was difficult to compose such true verse for other countries. Japan is further rich in the variety of vegetation, flowers, fish and shells.
　　Another remarkable thing is that the Japanese people are dexterous in the use of their hands, and therefore have an aptitude for fine art and industrial art. It is extremely astonishing that there are so many fine masterpiece of art to be found in this country although they were created during a history of incessant internal troubles.
　　In short, the whole land of Japan should be turned into a vast national park in the broad sense of speaking, and art in this country should be improved to the highest possible standard in order for Japan to fulfil her mission.
　　Her national policy should be the undertaking of the tourist trade and improvement of art and industrial art. As a result, Japan will not only be able to contribute to the improvement of ideas of all the peoples of the world, but also give refreshing pleasure and solace. Japan should try to attain the highest state of civilization and develop the highest form of arts.
　　At the present, all the people of the world feel most keenly the fear of war. The root cause of war is the strife consciousness which is deep-rooted in man's mind. This strife consciousness being born of barbarism, modern people have not cast off the surface of barbarism, although they like to advocate civilization. The way to rid people of this strife consciousness is to change their direction to an interest in art. In other words, redirect the progress of the world of strife to a peaceful world of art.
　　World peace enforced by the power of the sword is not lasting. It should fundamentally be realized through a revolution of idea which can only be performed through religion and art.
　　The reconstruction of Japan should be the reconstruction of a new Japan in which art predominates.






Some art objects owned
by the Hakone Art Museum

Yuna, women of the gay quarters (important cultural object). Beginning of the Edo Period (middle of the 17th century) author unknown. 2 ft. 3 ins.×2 ft. 6 ins.

　　Artists of Ukiyo-e paintings did not sign their names to their work.
　　Most of Ukiyo-e art depicts scenes which were well known to the common people. Yuna, shows a group of several young women who inhabited the gay or licensed quarters during the Edo Period.
　　The colors of the pointing are fresh, well depicting the general tone and atmosphere of such scenes. Yet there is a defined grace of line and symmetry of form which gives great attraction to the picture.





Cormorant Fishing by Hokusai Katsushika. (1760-1849). Length 3 ft. 2 ins. Width 1 ft. 2 ins.

　　This painting was completed when Hokusai Katsushika was sixty years of age and is regarded as one of his masterpieces. The work is painted on paper in color.






 　










Palace and Natural Scenery (important cultural object) by Yusho Kaihoku. Height 4 ft. Length 12 ft.

　　A pair of six-sectional folding screens. Momoyama Period (1574-1615). The Momoyama Period is called the Renaissance in Japan during which art in its gorgeous form flourished and the great masters such as Eitoku Kano, Tohaku Hasegawa, Yusho Kaihoku and others competed for art supremacy. Painted in black ink (sumi) with bold brushwork using paper as medium.










Deer in Summer. A pair of six-sectional folding screens by Seiho Takeuchi. (1864-1942). Height 5 ft. 8 ins. Length 12 ft. 3 ins.

　　These screens were displayed in an exhibition sponsored by the Ministry of Education in 1936. This work is one of Seiho Takeuchi's masterpieces in his late years. It strongly expresses the form of deer in summer time.









Wooden Cat by Gengen Sato (1888-　　). Height 1 ft. 4 ins.

　　This work was completed in 1944. This figure gives a strong impression of life. One can imagine that the cat will move.
　　In the history of Japanese sculpture since the Meiji Era, this work is said to be an epoch making masterpiece.










A pair of matched tea drinking cups by Ninsei Nonomura. Middle of the 17th century. Large cup diameter 3 ins. height 3 ins. Small cup diameter 2.7 ins. height 2.6 ins.

　　The author is known as the originator of Kyo Yaki (pottery traditional to Kyoto). The color of the large cup is silver and the small cup, gold.









Lacquer Writing Box by Honami Koetsu (important art object). Middle of the 17th century. Size 8×7.4×3.6 ins.

　　Carving on the lid shows a wood-cutter bearing logs and descending the mountain. The artist has cleverly made use of the curvature of the lid to create the impression of the down slope of the mountain.




　

　






Prince Shotoku at Two Years of Age. Height about 2 ft. 4 ins.

　　A wooden statue of Prince Shotoku, first son of Emperor Goyomei, at two years of age.
　　A famous patron of Buddhism in Japan, Prince Shotoku became a theme for sculpture and painting after his death. Since 1069, there have been not a few sculptures and paintings treating Prince Shotoku as the subject at two, five or seven years, and also during his adulthood.





Keito-ko (Cock's Head Pot) from Esshu, China. Height 20 ins.

　　Produced in the Six Dynasties in Esshu, China (600 A.D.), this pot is reputed to be the finest specimen of its kind in the world. This rare specimen was unearthed and found to be without damage. Such a large completely undamaged specimen has not been hitherto discovered.



 　




　

　









Beauties Under a Tree (important cultural object). T'ung Dynasty (about 1,300 Years old). Length 4 ft. 4 ins. Width 1 ft. 6 ins.

　　This picture was discovered in Turfan, Sin Kiang, China. One of the rare genre pictures of the T'ung Dynasty.





Ju Yao Vase. North Sung Dynasty. (900 years old). Height 9.5 ins. Diameter of top 2 ins.

　　This vase is reputed to be one of the masterpieces of Ju Yao Vase work. A pattern of camellias is precisely worked into the surface and glazed with olive color.








 　









Stone Image of a Bodhisattura from Gandhara, India (200 A. D.) Height 2 ft. 1 ins. Width 1 ft. 5 ins.

　　This image is reputed to be a rare masterpiece with its beautiful figure and exact and precise engraving.
