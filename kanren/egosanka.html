FOREWORD


　　Meishu-sama composed a large number of waka-(thirty-one syllable) poems on various subjects. From these, and also from Nidai-sama's poems, the following verses have been selected and compiled to be chanted at church services and on special occasions.
　　The vibration of the Japanese words is very high and beautiful. While they could not be translated into English literally, we believe the true meaning of each poem has been preserved.
　　lt is our hope that this work, which was transposed into the waka rhythm after translation, will fully convey Meishu-sama's teachings and feelings to our English-speaking members.
　　If you will read at least one page each day, you will find you are benefiting greatly by doing so.
